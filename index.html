Get to know about ProYouth Foundation

The future lies with the youth and we exist to inspire and support their dreams.

"Growing up in the small, one street town of Leandra in Mpumalanga many moons ago I always noticed how privileged I was because I had big brothers who had seen the world and the opportunities it presents. My brothers made sure that I was exposed to all possible opportunities for my growth and I believe that this has made me who I am proud to be today. Throughout the years, however, I have noticed with great concern that not everyone I grew up with has managed to make out of their lives the best they could and I figure that part of the reason could be they did not have the same privilege as I did. With this foundation, I wanted to provide big sisters and big brothers in the form of an organised mentorship program to those who do not have. This while providing a platform for the community to engage on issues that affect them and how they themselves can actively work out solutions. The foundation also aims to provide a platform that will connect those with resources in all forms who wish to give back to our community and positively impact young lives. I am Pro the development of young lives and this foundation exists to inspire and support their dreams." - Zandile

Our Mission

We believe in young people and we strive to create solid and self sustaining foundations for future generations by:

INSPIRING

We firmly believe that not even the sky is the limit and therefore aspire to ignite and inspire those who seek to create a lasting change to dream big dreams.

EMPOWERING

Our organized mentorship and skills development program seek to develop and empower young minds that see opportunities despite glaring challenges.

EDUCATING

As the saying goes “a child educated only at school is an uneducated child”, we seek to supplement school education with life skills that will produce whole young people.

Focus & Beneficiaries

Why focus on the youth you may ask..

Failing to invest in youth triggers substantial economic, social, and political costs resulting from negative outcomes such as early school drop-out, poor labour market entry, risky sexual behaviours, substance abuse, and crime and violence.
Young people require the right foundation from an early age. Prevention, in terms of investing now to avoid the onset of expensive social problems later, is far more efficient than remediation.
We believe in the bright future that is now in their hands enough to want to invest in order to secure it for the betterment and wellbeing of all communities.

2022 Planned Programmes

Monthly Mentorship Sessions

  • Academic mentorship sessions run every six weeks.
  • There are two target groups, Matriculants and then an open group for all other learners.
  • We also host dialogue sessions every quarter.
  • Academic mentorship sessions run every six weeks.
  • These sessions aim to address different areas of development for the young mind.
  • Facilitators are those who are experts in tackling such issues.
  • Issues tackled include mental health, sexuality, HIV and Aids related issues, Drugs abuse, bullying etc.
  • Project costs vary per session based on speakers etc.

ProMatrix Tertiary applications

  • Planned for July 2022
  • The aim is to assist 50 under privileged matriculants with and fund their university applications for 2023.
  • On the day we will provide laptops and WiFi where mentors will help the learners with their applications.
  • Applications cost +-R120 per institutions and we believe each learner must apply to at least 3.
  • These sessions aim to address different areas of development for the young mind.
  • Project Cost - R18,000.00

ProYoung Men Mentorship

  • Planned for July 2022
  • The aim is to provide a platform for "Big Brothers" to have a chat with and mentor young men over a hotdog or 2.
  • The event is planned to be a relaxed setting that will create a safe space for both.
  • We hope to impact at least 100 young men.
  • Project Cost - R13,500.00

ProYoung Women Mentorship

  • Planned for August 2022
  • The aim is to provide a platform for "Big Sisters" to have a chat with and mentor young men over high tea.
  • The event is planned to be a relaxed setting that will create a safe space for both.
  • We hope to impact at least 100 young women.
  • Project Cost - R15,000.00

Anti-Bullying Campaign

  • One of our highlights for the year, planned to run throughout October 2022.
  • The aim is to help eliminate the bullying pandemic in schools by finding creative ways to educate about kindness.
  • Workshops on kindness at schools with creative educational material
  • Learners come up with different creative campaigns every year to promote kindness.
  • Impact Primary and High School Learners
  • Project Cost - R10,000.00

Career Day

  • Planned for September 2022
  • The aim is to provide a platform for companies to showcase their offerings to young people, bringing closer the resources and opportunities to this rural community of youth.
  • On the day we will organize a venue and provide companies with exhibition stands. The program will include presentations from various stakeholders.
  • We will provide refreshments for all in attendance.
  • 350 learners in Grade 10 - 12 (two high schools)
  • Project Cost - R9,000.00

ProLeadership Camp

  • Planned for October 2022
  • A weekend long leadership boot camp aimed at practically honing the leadership skills from an early age.
  • The event is planned to be a relaxed setting that will create a safe space for both.
  • 50 learners who are are part of the ProYouth Mentorship Program.
  • Project Cost - R65,000.00

3rd Annual Essay Competition

  • Planned for November 2022
  • The aim of the competition is to force young people to interrogate the current status quo by responding to a thought provoking topic.
  • At a prize giving event, inspiring speakers are invited the winners present their case and parents get to witness what we do at the foundation.
  • Project Cost - R15,000.00

Investment Opportunities

ProYouth Foundation is an impact organization and we do not consider ourselves a charity. We believe in building sustainable partnerships with like minded entities and individuals for the better good.
We currently do not receive any funding or aid whatsoever from the government, or any government- related organization. Our success so far we has relied on the financial support from our founder and the generosity of the public at large to achieve our set goals.
We wish to extend an invitation to private companies to partner with us and support our course via their CSI initiatives. We can tailor make any investment to suit both of our needs, alternatively as a partner you can choose a project that most resonates with you.
